Listening

Introducción

En esta sección escucharás conversaciones de diferentes personas y situaciones, para que te familiarices con el idioma.
No te preocupes si no entiendes cada una de las palabras, ¡trata de comprender qué es lo que quieren decir!
Si lo deseas, puedes apoyarte con un diccionario para buscar las palabras clave en cada conversación.

¡Adelante, hazlo, tú puedes! Es uno de los ejercicios más recomendados para aprender a expresarte oralmente en el inglés.

Diálogos

Diálogo A

At the Construction Company / En la compañía constructora

At the Construction Company / En la compañía constructora

Diego: Hey, Mike. Can you help me carry this heavy wood?
 Mike: Sure. I can help you. Where should we put the wood?
 Diego: Let’s put the wood next to the cement.
 Mike: Okay. There you are.
 Diego: Hey Mike, have you seen my hammer?
 Mike: Yeah. It’s next to the box of nails.
 Diego: Thanks. What are you working on today?
 Mike: I’m installing the windows.
 Diego: That’s hard work. Do you have help?
 Mike: Yeah, my brother Tim is coming to help me today.
 Diego: Okay. See you later.
 Mike: Bye.

Diálogo B

At a Beauty Salon / En el salón de belleza

At a Beauty Salon / En el salón de belleza

Woman: Hi Evelyn.
Stylist: Hi Lucy. What can I do for you today?
Woman: Could you color my hair for me?
Stylist: Do you want me to cut and style it, too?
Woman: Yes, please. I’m going to a wedding today.
Stylist: Who’s getting married?
Woman: My niece, Margarita. She’s a very nice girl.
Stylist: Okay. Come with me to the sink and I’ll wash your hair.

Diálogo C

At School / En la escuela

At School / En la escuela

Mark: Hey Bob. Did you fix that desk in room 17?
Bob: Is it broken?
Mark: The teacher said the legs on the desk are loose.
Bob: I’d better fix that right away. I don’t want any children to get hurt.
Mark: I have to finish mopping the floor in the lunchroom.
Bob: What are you going to do after that?
Mark: I have to change some light bulbs in the library and then I’m going to sweep the floors.
Bob: Okay. I’m going to fix the desk and then wash the floor in the bathroom.
Mark: We certainly have a lot of work to do.
Bob: We sure do! See you later.
Mark: All right. Good-bye.
